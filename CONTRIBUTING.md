*Contributor Guidelines:*
We welcome all contributions to this project via GitHub issues and pull requests. Please follow these guidelines to make sure your contributions can be easily integrated into the project. As you start contributing to Megradea_Software, don't forget that your ideas are more important than perfect pull requests. We require that your contributions adhere to our Guiding principles and Code of Conduct.

- Contributions welcome from researchers, experts, and community members. This participatory approach allows multiple perspectives and expertise to be integrated into research from the start and ensures that peer-review happens for all outputs in an interactive manner, not just for the articles at the end.
- Researchers Assistance:
    - Conducting experiments as students, researchers, or research assistants;
    - Submit pull requests with clear descriptions.
    - Follow [style guide] for formatting.
- Expertise:
    - Providing feedback on results;
    - Creating tools essential for carrying research;
    - Providing data expertise;
    - Providing specialized expertise and support;

*Authorship Guidelines:*
- Conceptualizing the idea;
- Designing the project work;
- Acquisition, analysis, or interpretation of data for the work;
- Drafting the work or reviewing it critically;
- Final approval of the work.
- Managing community and project requirements;
- Entering into Agreements accountable for all aspects of the work;
- Writing and reviewing manuscripts.
- Authors contribute significantly and approve the final version.
- Order: alphabetical by last name.

*Acknowledging Contributors:*
- Contributors will be acknowledged in the following ways:
    - Footnotes or appendices in published papers;
    - A "Contributors" section on the project website or GitHub page;
    - A Thank You section in presentations or talks about the project;
    - Social media shoutouts for notable contributions.
- Types of contributions to be acknowledged:
    - Research assistance;
    - Expertise and feedback;
    - Community management and support;
    - Writing and editing;
    - Data provision and analysis.
